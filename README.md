## TL;DR

We systematically compare 6 tokenization strategies for Arabic LMs under 40M parameters.  
We find that BPC, validation loss, and generation quality produce three fundamentally different rankings.  
This challenges common assumptions about Arabic tokenization.

## Key Findings

- 🔥 BPC follows vocabulary size, not morphological quality
- 🔥 Morphological segmentation improves val_loss by ~14% but NOT BPC
- 🔥 BPE-16K produces best generation quality overall
- 🔥 Morph helps at small vocab (8K) but hurts at larger vocab (16K)
- 🔥 25M parameters are sufficient for coherent Arabic generation
- 🔥 Single-metric evaluation is dangerous — use multiple metrics

## Fair Pair Comparison

### Same params (24.8M): BPE-8K vs Morph-BPE-8K

- Morph wins val_loss by 13.5%
- BPE wins BPC by 0.27% (statistical tie)
- Morph wins generation quality
- "Smarter tokens ≠ fewer bits per character"

### Same params (28.9M): BPE-16K vs Morph-BPE-16K

- Morph wins val_loss by 14.7%
- BPE wins BPC by 1.7%
- BPE wins generation quality
- "At larger vocab, morphological segmentation is unnecessary"

## Hardware

Trained entirely on a single NVIDIA RTX 4050 (6GB VRAM).  
Google Colab T4 was used for parallel runs.  
Total training cost: **$0 (free compute only)**.

## Reproducibility

- All configs in `configs/`
- All training scripts in `src/`
- Same seed (`42`) across runs
- Full metrics CSVs and final summaries in `results/`

## Limitations

- Single seed (no confidence intervals yet)
- Single dataset (Arabic Wikipedia only)
- No external baseline comparison yet
- Preliminary human evaluation
- Model size varies across tokenizers (24.8M–37.1M)
